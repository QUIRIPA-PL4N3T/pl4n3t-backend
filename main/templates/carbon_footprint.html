{% translate 'Carbon Footprint' %}

{% blocktrans %} The carbon footprint is a measure of the total greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions, expressed in carbon dioxide equivalents (CO2e), that are produced directly or indirectly as a result of the activities of an organization, product or service. In the business context, carbon footprint measurement has several functionalities: {% endblocktrans %}

1. {% translate 'Identification of impacts' %}

{% blocktrans %} Carbon footprint measurement helps a company identify the environmental impacts of its operations in terms of GHG emissions, allowing it to identify areas for improvement and opportunities for reduction. {% endblocktrans %}

2. {% translate 'Risk management' %}

{% blocktrans %} Carbon footprinting also helps a company manage risks related to environmental regulation and customer and societal expectations regarding sustainability. {% endblocktrans %}

3. {% translate 'Cost savings' %}

{% blocktrans %} Identifying GHG emission reduction opportunities can help a company save costs in terms of energy and other resources. {% endblocktrans %}

4. {% translate 'Competitive differentiation' %}

{% blocktrans %} Measuring and reducing the carbon footprint can help a company differentiate itself from its competitors by demonstrating its commitment to sustainability and social responsibility. In summary, carbon footprint measurement is an important tool for a company's environmental management and sustainability, enabling impact identification, risk management, cost savings and competitive differentiation. {% endblocktrans %}
