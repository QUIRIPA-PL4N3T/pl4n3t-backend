{% translate 'Why does a company need to quantify its corporate footprint?' %}

{% translate 'Companies may quantify for several reasons:' %}

1. {% translate 'Social responsibility' %}

{% blocktrans %} By knowing their environmental impact, companies can take steps to minimize it and become more socially responsible. {% endblocktrans %}

2. {% translate 'Improved efficiency' %}

{% blocktrans %} By knowing their corporate footprint, companies can identify areas for improvement and take steps to become more efficient in their use of resources. {% endblocktrans %}

3. {% translate 'Competitiveness' %}

{% blocktrans %} Companies that demonstrate sustainability and reduced environmental impact are more attractive to consumers and may have a competitive advantage in the marketplace. {% endblocktrans %}

4. {% translate 'Regulatory compliance' %}

{% blocktrans %} In many countries, companies are required to comply with environmental regulations, and quantifying their corporate footprint is an important step towards compliance. In short, quantifying your corporate footprint (carbon, water and plastic) is a key tool for companies to understand their environmental impact, improve their efficiency, social responsibility, comply with environmental regulations and want to become carbon neutral. {% endblocktrans %}
