{% translate 'Different Levels of Carbon Footprints' %}

{% translate 'Corporate Footprint' %}

{% blocktrans %} The corporate carbon footprint is a measure of the amount of greenhouse gases an organization emits into the atmosphere as a result of its business operations. This includes direct emissions generated by the company (such as the burning of fossil fuels in vehicles or equipment) and indirect emissions (such as the generation of electricity consumed by the company and the production and transportation of materials or employee to their workplace). {% endblocktrans %}


{% blocktrans %} Measuring the corporate carbon footprint can help companies identify areas for improvement and set targets to reduce their greenhouse gas emissions in order to mitigate their environmental impact and comply with regulations and legal requirements related to climate change mitigation. {% endblocktrans %}

{% translate 'Events Footprint' %}

{% blocktrans %} The carbon footprint of events refers to the amount of greenhouse gases emitted into the atmosphere during an event, such as a conference, concert, festival or corporate meeting. The carbon footprint of an event is made up of several sources of emissions, including energy used for lighting and air conditioning, transportation of attendees, and production of materials used during the event. {% endblocktrans %}


{% blocktrans %} Measuring the carbon footprint of an event can help identify areas for improvement and establish action plans to reduce greenhouse gas emissions, which can help mitigate the environmental impact of the event and promote sustainability. {% endblocktrans %}

{% translate 'Product Footprint' %}

{% blocktrans %} The product carbon footprint refers to the amount of greenhouse gases emitted during the life cycle of a product, from its extraction, manufacture, distribution, use and final disposal. This footprint includes direct and indirect greenhouse gas emissions, such as the energy used to produce and transport the product, the emissions generated by the manufacture and use of the product, and the emissions associated with its final disposal. {% endblocktrans %}


{% blocktrans %} Measuring a product's carbon footprint can help companies and consumers better understand its environmental impact and make informed decisions on how to reduce the greenhouse gas emissions associated with its production and use. {% endblocktrans %}
