{% translate 'Water Footprint' %}

{% blocktrans %} The water footprint is a measure of the total volume of water used in the production of goods or services and its impact on the planet's water resources. Like the carbon footprint and plastic footprint, the water footprint is used to assess and reduce a company's environmental impact. The functionalities of the water footprint for a company may include: {% endblocktrans %}

1. {% translate 'Risk identification' %}

{% blocktrans %} Measuring the water footprint can help a company identify areas where there is a greater risk of water scarcity, which can be useful in making strategic decisions regarding water resource management. {% endblocktrans %}

2. {% translate 'Cost reduction' %}

{% blocktrans %} Reducing water consumption can result in cost savings for the company by reducing the need for potable water and the energy required to treat and deliver water. {% endblocktrans %}

3. {% translate 'Improved corporate image' %}

{% blocktrans %} Measuring and reducing the water footprint can help a company improve its corporate image and reputation in the marketplace, demonstrating its commitment to sustainability and responsible management of natural resources. {% endblocktrans %}

4. {% translate 'Regulatory compliance' %}

{% blocktrans %} En resumen, la huella hídrica es una medida del uso de agua de una empresa o actividad y su impacto en el medio ambiente. Las empresas pueden utilizar la huella hídrica para identificar áreas de uso excesivo de agua y tomar medidas para reducir su impacto en el medio ambiente y mejorar su sostenibilidad. {% endblocktrans %}
