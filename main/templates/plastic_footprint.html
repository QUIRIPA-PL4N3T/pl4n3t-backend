{% translate 'Plastic Footprint' %}

{% blocktrans %} The plastic footprint is a tool used to quantify the amount of plastic a company uses and the amount of plastic waste it generates throughout its supply chain and operations. This footprint allows companies to identify their environmental impacts and take measures to reduce their plastic use and better manage plastic waste. The functionalities of the plastic footprint for a company include: {% endblocktrans %}

1. {% translate 'Identification of impacts' %}

{% blocktrans %} Identification of critical points in the supply chain and operations where plastic is used and waste is generated. {% endblocktrans %}

2. {% translate 'Risk management' %}

{% blocktrans %} Evaluation of the company's environmental impact in terms of greenhouse gas emissions, energy consumption and use of natural resources, as well as the impact on air, water and soil pollution. {% endblocktrans %}

3. {% translate 'Cost savings' %}

{% blocktrans %} Identification of opportunities to reduce plastic use and improve plastic waste management, which can improve the company's efficiency and profitability. {% endblocktrans %}

4. {% translate 'Competitive differentiation' %}

{% blocktrans %} Development of sustainability and social responsibility strategies that enable the company to improve its image and reputation, attract environmentally conscious consumers and increase its competitiveness in the marketplace. In short, plastic footprinting is a valuable tool for companies that want to improve their sustainability and reduce their environmental impact. By quantifying their plastic use and waste, companies can identify opportunities to improve their resource management and reduce their impact on the environment. {% endblocktrans %}
